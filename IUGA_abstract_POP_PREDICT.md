**Development of the POP-PREDICT Score: A Machine Learning-Derived Prediction Calculator for Recurrence Risk in Pelvic Organ Prolapse Surgery**

Sullivan, Marie; Kim, Shihyun; El Haraki, Amr

**Introduction:** There are several calculators commonly used in medicine such as the Vaginal Birth After Cesarean calculator, the Caprini Score, or the CHA₂DS₂-VASc score. These calculators are widely adopted because they integrate multiple clinical variables into a standardized score, improving risk stratification, enhancing shared decision-making, and optimizing patient outcomes.

**Objective:** To develop and validate a clinical risk score for prolapse recurrence following pelvic organ prolapse (POP) surgery incorporating patient characteristics and surgical approach.

**Methods:** We performed a pooled analysis of four prospective clinical trials (ALTIS, BEST, EPACT, SASS) including 752 women undergoing POP surgery between 2017-2024. Surgical approaches included sacrocolpopexy (N=472), native tissue repair with uterosacral/sacrospinous ligament suspension (N=232), and colpocleisis (N=48). A composite recurrence outcome was defined as POP-Q stage ≥2, any POP-Q point ≥0, or bothersome bulge symptoms at follow-up. LASSO logistic regression was used for predictor selection and model development, validated using 5-fold cross-validation and leave-one-dataset-out (LODO) external validation. Regression coefficients were converted to a point-based clinical risk score.

**Results:** Mean age was 63.6 years (SD 11.2), BMI 28.0 kg/m² (SD 5.6), and 56.3% presented with stage 3 prolapse. Composite recurrence occurred in 28.1% of patients. The model achieved AUC 0.65, sensitivity 0.58, specificity 0.72, PPV 0.41, and NPV 0.83. External validation confirmed consistent performance (LODO AUC range: 0.57-0.67). The POP-PREDICT score (0-14 points) incorporates eight predictors: surgery type (0-2 points), baseline Ba point (0-3), parity (0-2), height (0-2), baseline C point (0-2), hormonal status (0-1), baseline Aa point (0-1), and race (0-1). The score stratifies patients into low (0-4 points, ~10% recurrence), moderate (5-7 points, ~25%), high (8-10 points, ~40%), and very high risk (11-14 points, ~55%) categories.

**Conclusion:** The POP-PREDICT score is a clinical tool for preoperative risk stratification that incorporates surgical approach and recurrence risk. This score facilitates shared decision-making by quantifying expected recurrence risk based on patient characteristics and planned procedure.

# POP-PREDICT Score

**Development and Validation of the POP-PREDICT Score: A Point-Based Clinical Risk Calculator for Recurrence Following Pelvic Organ Prolapse Surgery**

Sullivan, Marie; Kim, Shihyun; El Haraki, Amr

**Introduction:** Pelvic organ prolapse (POP) affects up to 50% of parous women and surgical repair carries a recurrence risk of 15–40% depending on the procedure performed and patient characteristics. Despite this, no validated point-based clinical risk calculator exists to quantify individual recurrence risk preoperatively. Tools such as the Caprini Score and CHA₂DS₂-VASc score demonstrate that integrating multiple clinical variables into a standardized score improves risk stratification, facilitates shared decision-making, and supports patient counseling. A validated preoperative risk calculator for prolapse recurrence would address an unmet need in urogynecologic practice.

**Objective:** To develop and validate a point-based clinical risk score for prolapse recurrence following POP surgery, incorporating preoperative patient characteristics and surgical approach.

**Methods:** We performed a pooled analysis of four prospective clinical trials (ALTIS, BEST, EPACT, SASS) including 752 women undergoing POP surgery between 2017–2024. Surgical approaches included sacrocolpopexy (N=472, 62.8%), native tissue repair including uterosacral and sacrospinous ligament suspension (N=232, 30.9%), and colpocleisis (N=48, 6.4%). Composite recurrence was defined as POP-Q stage ≥2, any POP-Q point ≥0, or bothersome vaginal bulge symptoms (PFDI-20 question 3 score ≥2) at any follow-up visit. The six-week postoperative visit was excluded from the recurrence assessment when longer-term follow-up data were available, and retained as the primary outcome observation when it was the only available follow-up. Standard logistic regression with backward stepwise selection by Akaike Information Criterion (AIC) was used for predictor selection. Model performance was assessed using full-cohort AUC, five-fold stratified cross-validation, and leave-one-dataset-out (LODO) external validation. Regression coefficients were converted to integer points using a clinically motivated scaling approach, with risk tiers defined empirically from the score distribution.

**Results:** Mean age was 62.9 years (SD 11.4) and BMI 28.1 kg/m² (SD 7.0). The majority presented with stage 3 prolapse (58.1%). Composite recurrence occurred in 27.9% of patients (N=210). The model selected four independent predictors of recurrence: surgery type, baseline anterior vaginal wall position (Ba point), baseline genital hiatus, and menopausal/hormonal status (Table 1). Greater anterior wall prolapse (OR 1.13 per cm, p=0.001), wider genital hiatus (OR 1.31 per cm, p<0.001), native tissue repair versus sacrocolpopexy (OR 1.48, p=0.032), and postmenopausal status without hormone replacement (OR 0.84 per level, p=0.124) were associated with recurrence; colpocleisis was associated with significantly lower recurrence risk versus sacrocolpopexy (OR 0.37, p=0.025). The model achieved AUC 0.67, sensitivity 0.66, specificity 0.60, PPV 0.39, and NPV 0.82 (Table 2). Five-fold cross-validation AUC was 0.64 (SD 0.07). LODO external validation AUC ranged from 0.55 to 0.66 across held-out trial datasets. The POP-PREDICT score (0–7 points) incorporates surgery type (0–2 points), baseline Ba point (0–2 points), genital hiatus (0–2 points), and hormonal status (0–1 point). The score stratifies patients into low (0–2 points, ~16% recurrence), moderate (3–5 points, ~28%), and high risk (6–7 points, ~51%) categories (Table 3).

**Conclusion:** The POP-PREDICT score is the first validated point-based clinical tool for preoperative risk stratification of prolapse recurrence. The score integrates four readily available preoperative variables — surgical approach, anterior wall Ba point, genital hiatus, and hormonal status — into a 0–7 point scale. Discriminative performance was consistent across internal cross-validation and external leave-one-dataset-out validation (AUC 0.64–0.67). This tool supports individualized preoperative counseling, shared decision-making, and identification of high-risk patients who may benefit from alternative surgical approaches or closer postoperative surveillance.
